Light Pollution Statistics

Light pollution, the excessive or misdirected artificial light in the environment, is a growing concern in the Philippines, particularly in urbanized regions. Here are some key statistics highlighting its impact:

Metro Manila

The capital region exhibits significant light pollution, with average sky brightness levels around 23.4 magnitudes per square arcsecond, indicating a highly light-polluted night sky.

2024 New Year's Eve Celebration

The graph illustrates the real-time PM₂.₅ concentrations in various sampling locations in the Philippines over a 24-hour period from December 31, 2023 (12 PM) to January 1, 2024 (12 PM). A sharp and significant spike in PM₂.₅ levels is observed around midnight (January 1), particularly in San Jose Del Monte, Bulacan (light blue) and Calumpit, Bulacan (gray), likely due to New Year's Eve celebrations, such as fireworks and firecrackers. Other areas, including Davao City and Tagaytay, Cavite, show relatively lower PM₂.₅ levels throughout the period, indicating less pollution. After the midnight peak, PM₂.₅ concentrations gradually decline in all locations, but elevated levels persist for a few hours into the early morning of January 1, 2024. (Manila Observatory, 2024)

Mindanao

Research indicates that light pollution in Mindanao has been expanding, influencing local wildlife such as bat populations. The spread of artificial lighting disrupts natural habitats and behaviors, posing ecological challenges.
